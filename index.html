Bullet camera

Bullet cameras, also known as lipstick cameras, are cylindrical. Their shape is rather bullet-like iconic. They have a capacity for long-distance observing.
They come with a viewing angle of about 80°. They are usually placed outdoor above the ceiling or on the walls. The protective casings of the bullet cameras make it weather and dustproof.
ideal uses:

  • Parking lots, outside premises
  • Long-distance surveillance

Dome camera

Dome cameras are typically used indoors to prevent vandalism. They are dome-shaped and quite discreet in appearance. One can not be sure which direction the camera is facing.
It has got Vandal-resistant features and IR night vision. Retail stores typically use dome cameras. They come with a fixed viewing angle like 100°.and 360-degree rotation.
ideal uses:

  • restaurants and hotels
  • Offices and retails shops

C-mount Camera

C- mount cameras use special lenses. It enables the CCTV to cover distance beyond 40 ft. They are quite bulky and quite obvious. Detachable lenses in the C-mount cameras can zoom in and zoom out without losing focus.
Its bulky appearance deters criminal activities visibly. It has got a Rugged casing which works in cold weather.
Ideal uses:

  • Superzoom monitoring
  • Manufacturing or logistics industries

Infrared/ Night Vision Camera

Infrared technology has enabled capturing footage in poor lighting and dark conditions. It captures color footage during daytime and black and white during the night.
They can capture clearly amidst fog and smoke. They are vital for security issues. You can get reliable recordings in absolute darkness, as well.
Ideal uses:

  • Banks, farms and manufacturing plants.

Day / Night CCTV Camera

Day/ Night cameras are used for 24/ 7. They do not require infrared technology. Instead, they use an extra-sensitive imagining chip to capture recording at night.
No matter the surrounding is well lit or not they operate effectively,
ideal uses:

  • All-time surveillance
  • Low light conditioning
